The Cuban Religions

Cuban religion is a vibrant mix of Roman Catholicism, Afro-Cuban traditions like Santería, and a growing secular population. While Catholicism remains the most widespread faith, many Cubans practice Santería, a spiritual system blending Yoruba deities (orishas) with Catholic saints. Other Christian denominations, such as Protestants, are present but smaller. A significant portion of the population identifies as non-religious, reflecting Cuba’s secular history. Religious life in Cuba is deeply tied to culture, with rituals, festivals, and beliefs often blending sacred and everyday traditions.

Cultural and Spiritual Traditions
  • Santería blends Catholic saints with Yoruba deities (orishas), involving rituals, drumming, and offerings.
  • Espiritismo (spiritism) and other folk practices are also common, especially in rural areas.
  • Religious festivals like the Feast of Our Lady of Charity (La Virgen de la Caridad del Cobre) are widely celebrated, even by non-practicing Cubans.
  • Religion often coexists with cultural identity, more than strict doctrine.
