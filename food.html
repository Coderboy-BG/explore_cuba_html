The Cuban Food

Cuban food is a vibrant fusion of Spanish, African, Caribbean, and indigenous Taíno influences. It’s known for its hearty ingredients, bold seasonings, and simple, satisfying preparation. Staples include rice and beans (Moros y Cristianos), plantains, yuca, pork, chicken, and seafood, often seasoned with garlic, onion, cumin, oregano, and citrus marinades. Meals are typically slow-cooked or fried, emphasizing natural flavors over heavy sauces. Dining is family-centered, with lunch as the main meal, and street food and paladares (private restaurants) offering authentic local experiences.


Famous Cuban Dishes
  • Ropa Vieja: Shredded beef in tomato sauce with peppers and onions
  • Arroz con Pollo: Chicken and rice cooked with spices and vegetables
  • Puerco Asado: Marinated roast pork, a festive favorite
  • Tostones: Twice-fried green plantains, crispy and savory
  • Yuca con Mojo: Cassava root with garlic-citrus sauce
  • Pan con lechon: Ham, roast pork, Swiss cheese, pickles, and mustard on Cuban bread
